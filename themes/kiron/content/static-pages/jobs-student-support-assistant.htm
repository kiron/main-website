Student Support Assistant

Start: September 2016 - Office: Berlin/Munich - fulltime

Kiron Open Higher Education (gGmbH) is a non-profit organization founded in 2015 with a mission to remove the barriers refugees face in accessing higher education. Through an innovative model of blended learning, Kiron offers tailor-made curricula of MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) through renowned educational platforms like coursera and edx so that refugees can start studying, regardless of their asylum status. Courses are mostly provided in English, completely free of charge for the students and all accessible via our learning platform Kiron Campus.

Through strong partnerships with accredited universities worldwide, students have the opportunity to finish their program with a regular bachelor’s degree. Today, a team of 40 employees receives further support from about 400 volunteers worldwide and a competence pool consisting of experts from relevant fields, such as professors, psychologists and business specialists.

Your opportunities and responsibilities at Kiron:.

  • Working with our Help Desk and emPower Programme teams.

  • Answering student’s requests for assistance.

  • Supporting the matching process between volunteers and Kiron students.

  • Assisting regional groups in the establishment of Kiron’s student services.

  • Supporting students in their transfer to partner universities.

Your set of skills:

  • You hold a bachelor’s degree in the  field of social sciences, educational sciences, psychology or other relevant studies.

  • You are experienced working with refugees and (volunteer) teams. 

  • You are a great communicator and love to facilitate community building.

  • You are a self-starter, adaptive & resilient and have an entrepreneurial mind-set.

  • You are fluent in English and German and are proficient with the Microsoft Office Package and Google Apps

  • You share the vision of free education worldwide for refugees

Our offer:

  • The experience to work with a highly committed, international and interdisciplinary (volunteer) team.

  • The atmosphere of an innovative and fast-growing social startup with several stakeholders (refugees, corporations, NGOs, higher education and public sector institutions).

  • Regular team building as well as representative events, mentoring, trainings and workshops.

  • A competitive salary within the social sector.

  • Flexible working hours (incl. Home Office) at our Berlin or Munich location.
