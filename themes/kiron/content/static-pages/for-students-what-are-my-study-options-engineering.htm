Engineering

Do you find maths to be a breeze? Do technical systems and technologies fascinate you? Then you should think about studying Engineering.

The Kiron Engineering Study Track lets you acquire the knowledge of scientific and technical fundamentals in different fields of engineering and teaches you a technical  approach to solving real-life problems. Upon completion of the core modules within the engineering field of your choice, you can choose to specialize in different subjects and target a specific Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, Chemical or Environmental Engineering degree at a partner university.

Interdisciplinary skills in project management, quality management and law are also covered to different extents in engineering curricula. After successfully completing one of the engineering tracks, you will 

  • gain the fundamental skills and mindset that any engineer must have in the first place,

  • be able to analyze real-world problems and approach them analytically,

  • be able to develop engineering models and apply them to solve actual engineering problems.

Where will the degree take you?

A degree in any of the following Engineering studies opens up a vast variety of possibilities both in academia and in industry:

  • Mechanical Engineering involves the design, production and operation of machinery; it opens the path towards academic and industrial positions in fields such as automotive, aerospace, manufacturing, energy and robotics.

  • Electrical Engineering involve the science and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism; it opens the path towards academic and industrial positions in fields such as energy, electronics, manufacturing, automotive and telecommunication.

  • Civil Engineering involves the design, construction and maintenance of the physical and naturally built environment; it opens the path towards academic and industrial positions in fields such as construction, infrastructure, municipal or urban planning. 

  • Chemical Engineering involves the production, transformation and transportation of chemicals, materials and energy; it opens the path towards fields such as energy, biotechnology and nanotechnology.

  • Environmental Engineering involves the science and technologies that deal with environmental preservation and access to natural resources; it opens the path towards fields such as environmental analysis, waste management, water resources management, municipal or urban planning.
