Engineering

Do you find maths to be a breeze? Do technical systems and technologies fascinate you? Then you should think about studying Engineering.

The Kiron Engineering Department lets you acquire the knowledge of scientific and technical fundamentals in different fields of engineering and teaches an engineering approach to solving real-life problems.

Key Outcomes

Through your engineering studies at Kiron, you will

  • gain the fundamental skills and mindset that any engineer must have in the first place,

  • be able to analyze real-world problems and approach them analytically,

  • be able to develop engineering models and apply them to solve actual engineering problems.

Where will the degree take you?

Kiron is currently offering Mechanical Engineering Study Track for Kiron Students and planning to start offering others soon. Mechanical Engineering involves the design, production and operation of machinery; it opens the path towards academic and industrial positions in fields such as automotive, aerospace, manufacturing, energy and robotics. A degree in Mechanical Engineering opens up a vast variety of possibilities both in academia and in industry. Kiron Mechanical Engineering Study Track starts with scientific fundamentals in subjects such as mathematics, physics and chemistry. These are followed by more technical foundations in mechanics, materials science, thermodynamics, electronics and manufacturing technologies. Interdisciplinary skills in project management, quality management and business administration are also covered to different extents.
