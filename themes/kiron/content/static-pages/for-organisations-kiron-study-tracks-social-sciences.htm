Social Sciences

Political Science

Political Science deals with the process of political decision-making in modern societies. In this study track, our students will learn how political processes work in states, private companies, and civil societies. Political Scientists have the competence to use scientific theories and methods to identify and analyze political coherences and problems. 

As parts of the Kiron core curriculum in political sciences our students will gain foundational competencies in fields such as sociology, globalization, international development, and policy analysis. 

Key Learning Outcomes

After successfully completing the Political Science core curriculum, our students

  • Understand what Political Science is about and what it can be used for

  • Have foundational knowledge in both political theory and research practice in Political Science

  • Have a critical, theoretically profound and scientifically driven perspective on global political challenges 

Social Work

There is an increasing interest and understanding of the question how social sciences can be used to solve practical problems. Our study track “Social Work” will allow our students to become an academically trained, hands-on expert in solving social problems. With a degree in Social Work, they will be empowered to act in ways that are scientifically profound, responsible and professional.

Social Work programs may have different focuses. The Kiron core curriculum provides students with foundational competencies through the following fields of human sciences, social justice, interculturality and diversity. 

Key Learning Outcomes

After successfully completing the Social Work core curriculum, our students

  • Understand what Social Work is about and what it is good for

  • Have foundational knowledge of social theories related to Social Work as an academic discipline

  • Have a basic understanding of Social Work methods

  • Particularly have a foundational understanding of Health as a Social Work field
