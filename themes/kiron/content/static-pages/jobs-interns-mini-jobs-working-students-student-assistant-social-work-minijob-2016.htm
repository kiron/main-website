Student Assistant in Curriculum Development Social Work Study Track (m/f)

to support our operations in Berlin or work remotely from any location.

Kiron Open Higher Education (gGmbH) is a non-profit organization founded in 2015 with a mission to remove the barriers refugees face in accessing higher education. Through an innovative model of blended learning, Kiron offers tailor-made curricula of MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) through renowned educational platforms like coursera and edx so that refugees can start studying, regardless of their asylum status. Courses are mostly provided in English, completely free of charge for the students and all accessible via our learning platform Kiron Campus.

Through strong partnerships with accredited universities worldwide, students have the opportunity to finish their program with a regular bachelor’s degree. Today, a team of 40 employees receives further support from about 400 volunteers worldwide and a competence pool consisting of experts from relevant fields, such as professors, psychologists and business specialists.

Your opportunities and responsibilities at Kiron:

  • Assist the existing team in the development of Kiron’s Social Work core curriculum by refining module content and structure, and finding high quality MOOCs (Massive Open Online Course) which cover the specified Learning Outcomes
  • Match Kiron modules and MOOCs with modules and courses at our partner universities for our Social Work study track to help Kiron achieve curriculum equivalence
  • Maximize the probability that our students receive recognition, from partner universities, for their studies through your work on the matching process.
  • Finalize this into a complete Learning Agreement that can be signed by our academic partners

Your skillset:

  • Currently enrolled in a Social Work related Master’s Program
  • Ability to do self-paced, autonomous work
  • Care and rigor, excellent time management, responsibility and reachability
  • Fluency in English (our primary working language) and at least a working knowledge of German is required
  • Ideally based in Berlin

Our offer:

  • 450€/month salary (10€/hour x 45 hours)
  • Flexible working hours
  • The opportunity to shape a social enterprise with your creative input and have a tangible impact on our educational offering
  • Work within the vibrant atmosphere of an innovative, fast-growing social startup
  • Join a highly motivated, international, and interdisciplinary team
  • Help refugees in Germany, and around the world, through your work on developing a world class curriculum that is accessible to all
