HOW TO OBTAIN A FUNDING

Students with a refugee status can face several barriers to access higher education. In many cases, the financial burden is a particular challenge to become a student and to live a student life. This section provides information about funding programmes and initiatives supporting and facilitating refugees in their studies with financial aid and advice. The following information is based on European resettlement network.

ADVICE, GUIDANCE & INFORMATION

Foundation for Refugee Students UAF

The Foundation for Refugee Students UAF provides advice, support and financial assistance to refugees and asylum seekers wishing to study in the Netherlands. Since 2009, the UAF has been running a project specifically for higher educated refugees resettled in the Netherlands

Find more about the Foundation for Refugee Students UAF


Refugee Support Network

London based charity Refugee Support Network supports young people affected by displacement and crisis to access and progress in education. They also run the ‘Higher Education Programme’, a specialist support service for young refugees and asylum seekers who are having problems accessing higher education. A report on the programme gives valuable insights and is available for free download.

SPONSORSHIPS & SCHOLARSHIPS

Otto Benecke Foundation

The Otto Benecke Foundation is a non-profit organisation launched in 1965 by the German students’ associations. The foundation runs educational programmes with the aim of integrating immigrants and refugees into German society.

Find out more about the Otto Benecke Foundation support programme


Albert Einstein German Academic Refugee Initiative (DAFI)

The Albert Einstein German Academic Refugee Initiative (DAFI) is a scholarship assistance programme for refugees funded by the German Government and implemented worldwide by UNHCR. The programme grants scholarships to refugees at universities, colleges and polytechnics in their host countries. In 2008, the programme also started offering education opportunities in their countries of origin to refugees considering repatriation.

Find out more about Albert Einstein German Academic Refugee Initiative (DAFI)


Hegg Hoffet Fund for Displaced Women Graduates

The Hegg Hoffet Fund for Displaced Women Graduates of the Geneva-based International Federation of University Woman assists graduate women who have been displaced as a result of war, political upheaval or other serious emergencies. The Fund provides short term grants for refresher courses for re-entry into the candidates’ professional field, and for language training and other courses to assist with integration into their new countries. In addition to financial assistance, IFUW’s national and local groups also provide the graduate refugees with moral support to help them to adjust to life in a different country.

Find out more about the Hegg Hoffet Fund for Displaced Women Graduates

Find out more about the International Federation of University Women


Entraide Universitaire

The Entraide Universitaire (French University Student Services Association) is an association offering financial help to refugee students in France who are not eligible for a regular scholarship.

Find out more about the Entraide Universitaire


Academic Relations between Asia and Europe (AREAS)

The Academic Relations between Asia and Europe (AREAS) provides scholarships for students and researchers from the Third Countries targeted by the project (Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Mongolia, Sri Lanka, Thailand, The Philippines, Vietnam) in order to study, do research, training or teaching at one of the EuropeanPartner Universities (Host Universities). Scholarships are also open to candidates with refugee (TG3) status.

Find out more about the Academic Relations between Asia and Europe (AREAS) scholarship
