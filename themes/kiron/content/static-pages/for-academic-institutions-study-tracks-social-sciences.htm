Social Sciences

Political Science 

The Kiron Political Science core curriculum offers fundamentals of Political Science by providing foundational knowledge and skills in Political Theory, Political Research and an introduction to the main subdisciplines, Comparative Politics, International Relations and Policy Analysis’. Those are being rounded by foundational studies in Political Economy and Political Sociology. In order to provide students with contents that are relevant to a globalised world and foster their applicability in a wide range of regions.

Example: 

Name: Research Methods and Statistics 

Workload: 9 CP

Description

This module provides students with an understanding of systematic political science research and its application. The focus lies on quantitative and qualitative research and their methodologies. The lessons deal with the research process, research design, selection process, sampling design and various methods of data collection and analysis for Political Science.  The module also provides an introduction to statistics for political science research. 

Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this module students: 

  • understand the subjects, objectives and methods relevant to Political Science research 

  • understand the difference between qualitative and quantitative methods in Political Science 

  • are able appropriately apply Political Science Research Methods

  • are able to apply statistical methods for analyzing political and social problems and developments 

MOOCs

  • Basic Statistics, by University of Amsterdam

  • Qualitative Research Methods, by University of Amsterdam

  • Quantitative Methods by University of Amsterdam

  • Framework for Data Collection and Analysis by University of Maryland, College Park

  • Questionnaire Design for Social Surveys, by University of Michigan

  • Statistical Thinking for Data Science and Analytics, by Columbia University


Social Work

The Kiron Social Work core curriculum offers broad theoretical and basic scientific foundations in Social Work as an academic discipline. Studies in related sciences such as sociology and psychology provide students with an understanding of Social Work’s theoretical embeddedness. While offering basic knowledge in the field of education and social justice the Kiron core curriculum puts the strongest emphasis on Health Studies by offering 2 related modules: Health Studies I+II. Moreover the module Social Management’ teaches key competencies for managing social projects. 

Example: 

Name: Health Studies I

Workload: 7 CP

Description

This module introduces students to the social construction and embeddedness of health and disease in societies. It thereby provides students with a socially critical perspective on health and disease. Moreover students get to know main social institutions involved in treating health and disease.

Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this module students:

  • Have foundational knowledge about social empirical research on health 

  • Have foundational knowledge about the main institutional health care providers

  • Have foundational knowledge about population health 

  • Understand contemporary regional and world health issues

  • Understand the social meaning of health/ disease

  • Understand the social construction of health/ disease

MOOCs

  • An introduction to population health, by University of Manchester

  • Healthcare Delivery Providers, by University of Minnesota

  • Community Change in Public Health, by Johns Hopkins University

  • The Social Context of Mental Health and Illness, by University of Toronto

  • Health and Society (archived), by Harvard University

  • Global Adolescent Health, by The University of Melbourne
