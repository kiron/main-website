EmPower Program.

The EmPower Program is designed specifically to empower refugees by targeting two of the challenges they face: integration into the new society and job market, and access to psychosocial support. The Program has three branches:

Buddy Program: We partner with volunteer students from local universities who act as local contacts for Kiron students, offering support and advice and introducing Kiron students to the student life in their city.

Mentoring Program: Our mentors help students prepare for their future career, serve as role models, and give insights into organizations. We cooperate with non-profit Kwantum Mentoring Ltd, which provides a team of dedicated, trained and multilingual mentors.

Psychosocial Support:   Exposure to violence, disaster, and separation from loved ones, in conjunction with lack of access to services, can all have immediate and long-term consequences for the psychosocial well-being of refugees. Therefore, our students have both online and offline access to our team of professional psychologists.

Careers Guidance.

Our ultimate goal is to pave our student’s way into the job market. We want to empower people to make their own living, while making best use of their skills and knowledge.

We therefore help our students to gain valuable practical experience through internships, and support them in finding permanent employment in their area of interest once they leave us. Through a partnership with a recognized job platform we connect our students with employers, and are constantly working to build our strong network of companies who can provide internships and employment to our students.
