Business & Economics

The Business & Economics core curriculum offers the best of both worlds: Through our Economics modules, students will understand on a theoretical level how modern economies function and integrate into a larger social framework, while in our Business modules, students will learn on a practical level how to lead a business organization. Going through our Business & Economics track and completing their degree at a partner university equips students with a set of both intellectual and hands-on skills that make them particularly valuable job market candidates.

An exemplary module in the Business & Economics track is “Marketing”.

Example: 

Name: Marketing

Workload: 6 CP

Description

This module teaches the core concepts and tools of marketing, including key topics such as market research and its importance for company strategy, brand strategy, pricing, integrated marketing communication and social media communication. In doing so, the module introduces students to the marketing cycle, from identifying a customer base, conceptualizing and implementing an effective marketing strategy, to monitoring and evaluating one’s marketing efforts.

Learning Outcomes

After successfully completing this module, students will be able to:

  • review central marketing instruments
  • apply marketing instruments to a business environment
  • develop a marketing plan/ mix
  • examine strategically challenges to marketing in a globalized world economy
  • indicate the ethical implications of various marketing instruments
