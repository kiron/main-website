Business & Economics

The Business & Economics core curriculum offers the best of both worlds: Through our Economics modules, students will understand on a theoretical level how modern economies function and integrate into a larger social framework, while in our Business modules, students will learn on a practical level how to lead a business organization. Going through our Business & Economics track and completing their degree at a partner university equips students with a set of both intellectual and hands-on skills that make them particularly valuable job market candidates.

An exemplary module in the Business & Economics track is “Politics & Economics”, which blends economic analysis with applications to current real-world challenges.

Example: 

Name: Politics & Economics

Workload: 5 CP

Description

This module is designed to introduce students to those parts of economics that bear an especially close connection to pressing social issues. Students will be familiarized with exemplary political debates that have an economic dimension, or whose analysis can be helpfully furthered by using the tools of economics. Likewise, they will be sensitized to some of the political, social and ethical dimensions to economic decisions. Possible case studies discuss economic policy-making, economic growth, distributive justice, social choice, behavioral economics and the ethics of nudge, financial crises, energy and environmental economics, happiness research or the ethics and economics of future generations.

Learning Outcomes
After successfully completing this module, students

  • will be able to identify political, social and ethical dimensions to economic decisions and embed the assessment of economic decisions in a wider social context

  • will better understand the policies being discussed around them and in the media and be able to develop their own informed opinion about these policies in light of their economic, political, social and ethical implications

  • will be able to assess both the limitations and benefits of the tools of economics in discussing social problems

  • apply insights from economics to case studies that are based on real-world challenges

  • will be better equipped  to communicate the economic aspects of social problems to non-specialists
