Computer Science

Computing and computer technology are part of just about everything that touches our lives, from the cars we drive, to the movies we watch, to the numerous ways we all interact with each other. Whether you want to create the next hot application or want to make a positive difference in the world by developing innovative technology in science, studying computing will give you a foundation of knowledge, problem solving skills and habits of logical thinking that will provide you a competitive edge and a career that will never be obsolete.  

The Kiron Computer Science curriculum gives a profound introduction to the mathematical and engineering basics of the discipline and equips you with the tools and knowledge necessary for a successful research, industrial and entrepreneurial career in computing.

Key Learning Outcomes

A set of "core" courses gives you a thorough foundation in analysis, linear algebra, programming principles, algorithms, complexity management, and computing systems. It also provides considerable flexibility in course selection around topics such as Cryptography, Artificial Intelligence, Statistics and Web Programming. Interdisciplinary skills in business administration and law are also covered to complement the computer science curriculum. 

 After successfully completing the computer science track, you will 

  • be able to analyze a complex problem, then develop and implement an efficient solution. 

  • gain a profound understanding of both hardware and software

  • be able to work independently and in a structured manner 

  • be able to develop comprehensive computer programs as part of a team

  • have knowledge of contemporary issues related to Computer Science & Engineering.

Where will the degree take you?

Computer scientists have excellent career prospects in many occupational fields as information technology is needed in almost all areas. There are great job opportunities for computer scientists such as researcher, developer, programmer or consultant within various industries like automotive, mechanical, medical, financial, and research (just to mention a few).
